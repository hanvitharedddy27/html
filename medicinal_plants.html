Medicinal Plants: Tulsi (Holy Basil)

1. Cultivation of Tulsi

Climate: Warm climates with temperatures between 20°C and 30°C.

Soil: Well-drained, fertile loamy soil with a pH of 6 to 7.5.

Watering: Moderate watering twice a week, avoiding over-watering.

Harvesting: Harvest after 2-3 months of planting, pluck leaves when the plant is about 12-15 inches tall.

2. Uses of Tulsi

  • Medicinal: Immunity booster, treats respiratory issues, and manages diabetes.
  • Spiritual: Sacred in Hindu culture, believed to bring positivity.
  • Culinary: Used in teas, soups, salads, and other dishes.

3. Side Effects of Tulsi

  • Blood-thinning effects; caution for people on anticoagulants.
  • Possible impact on fertility in large doses.
  • May cause hypoglycemia in people with diabetes.

4. Role in Diagnosing and Curing Diseases

  • Anti-cancer properties: Prevents tumor formation and reduces radiation side effects.
  • Cardiovascular health: Lowers cholesterol and blood pressure.
  • Respiratory health: Relieves coughs, asthma, and bronchitis.
  • Antioxidant effects: Reduces oxidative stress and detoxifies the body.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Relieves pain and swelling, especially in arthritis.
